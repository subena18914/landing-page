PRODUCT DESCRIPTION

The First benefit of bamboo sheets is the sensual feel on your skin. The sheets have "good hand"! That's the term that fabric pundits use to describe the way that a fabric conforms to your body. It's the way the fabric drapes across your shoulders, arms and legs. The fabric doesn't cling, it just lies nicely. Bamboo sheets caress the body and just feel good.

If that wasn't enough reason to buy bamboo, there's a bunch of other reasons. 2) Bamboo is antibacterial. 3) It is organic. 4) It is hypoallergenic. 5) It is environmentally friendly. 6) And, it is absorbent.

Second: Bamboo has adapted to growing where the temperature and humidity are high and everything, including bacteria and molds, grows quickly. In order to survive, the plant developed antibacterial and anti fungal properties. These properties are reported to exist in the finished product. The sheets naturally resist the growth of bacteria and fungus. And, the fabric is a deterrent to dust mites.

Third: Bamboo has grown for aeons without any help from people. It's adapted to live where it does and can compete for space among its neighbors. It doesn't need herbicides to kill weeds, or insecticides to kill bugs or fertilizers to enrich the soil. It does just fine without these dangerous chemicals. It's nice to know that the environment wasn't poisoned so you could get a good night's sleep.

Fourth: Bamboo fabric doesn't induce allergies. It is naturally hypoallergenic. Bamboo fabric is easy to launder and wears well. It doesn't "pill up". It doesn't need perfumed fabric softeners. It's natural, so you won't become allergic to your bed.

Fifth: The most common alternative to bamboo is cotton. Cotton is a very destructive plant to grow. It requires twenty-five percent of all the herbicide manufactured to grow this one-crop! Plus it requires vast quantities of insecticide to kill boll weevils and other voracious insects. On top of that, the plant is destroyed, the fields are burned and the soil is turned over, each year. The ground is then vulnerable to wind and rain. Where are the birds and bees supposed to live in an environment that is consistently destroyed? What is left for the animals?

Bamboo cultivation is better, only the ripe bamboo shoots are harvested. The forest of younger shoots are still there, the plants stays where they are. The soil isn't turned, the forest isn't destroyed. The birds and bugs still have a home. The soil doesn't dry out, erode and get blown around. The wind and rain wash the forest. It stays put. It's been going on for aeons and will continue to do so.

Sixth: Bamboo is three times as absorbent as cotton. This makes it great as bedding, towels, bathrobes and clothing. The towels and bathrobes soak up water. The soft, bamboo fabric wicks water away from your skin in the bedding and in socks, shirts, blouses and pants. It's a wonderful fabric that is used to make sensual products.
